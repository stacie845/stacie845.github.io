Kirby

Kirby is an 8-inch, pink, spherical creature with short arms, red feet, and big eyes, known for his ability to inhale enemies and copy their powers to gain new abilities

KIRBY'S CREATION

Kirby was created by Masahiro Sakurai at HAL Laboratory in 1990, initially as a simple placeholder graphic for the Game Boy game Kirby's Dream Land. The developers grew fond of the "blob" character, which was temporarily named "Popopo," and it became the game's protagonist. The name was changed to Kirby to appeal to Western audiences, partially in honor of American lawyer John Kirby.

KIRBY'S BACKSTORY

Kirby's backstory is revealed primarily through the video game series' lore, the Kirby: Right Back at You anime, and other media, where he's depicted as a naive, childlike warrior from Dream Land with unknown origins who protects his home from evil by inhaling enemies and copying their abilities. His character was initially a placeholder for the first Kirby's Dream Land game but became the star after the developer, Masahiro Sakurai, grew fond of the design. While his true origins are often mysterious and open to fan interpretation, some lore suggests he may be connected to the benevolent cosmic entity Void.
